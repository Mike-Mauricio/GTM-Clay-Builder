## Create Multi-Step Sequence Messaging with Twain

In the last lesson we covered **AI Snippets**. These are a modular, high-control approach to generating content where you can customize every part of a message. But what if you don't want or need to customize every step?

What if you're looking for a **lower-friction** way to start your outbound sequences… and just want the AI to generate full email copy for you?

That's exactly what the **Twain enrichment** is for.

## 🧩 How Twain Structures Your Messaging

Instead of asking AI to generate a single block of text, Twain walks the model through a **multi-step process**, typically including:

- Subject Line
- Intro Line
- Body or Value Prop
- Call to Action (CTA)
- PS or Signature (optional)

And it can automatically use the enriched data in your Clay table, like:

- Company name and description
- Job title
- Recent funding
- Tech stack
- Or any other signals you've pulled in

All that data gets plugged into a structured template behind the scenes.

## ✅ When to Use Twain

Twain is a good solution when:

- You want full email copy, not just snippets
- You need to ensure you're not overloading AI with too much unstructured data
- You still need to customize tone, length, or structure
- You want outputs that are clean, modular, and easy to edit

## 🛠️ Step-by-Step Setup Process

Here's how to set up your sequence:

1. **Choose your sequence type:**
	- Select a pre-defined sequence (1-4 steps) from the dropdown menu
		- If you have a Twain account, you can use your own sequence by selecting your Twain account and entering a sequence ID
2. **Configure your prompt parameters:**
	- Add your prospect description
		- Define your outreach signal
		- Describe your ideal customer profile
		- State the problem you're solving
		- And more…
3. **Add your prospect information:**
	- Enter first name, last name, and social media profile URL for each prospect
		- Don't have social media profile URLs? Use Claygent to find them using prospect emails
4. **Map and execute:**
	- Map Twain's multi-step messages to your Clay columns
		- Run the table to generate your sequences

## 🤔 AI Snippets vs. Twain: When to Use Each

This might leave you wondering – when should you use AI snippets vs Twain?

- Use **AI Snippets** when you want full control and flexibility at the sentence level
- Use **Twain** when you want a **faster**, more **structured** way to generate outbound messaging at scale

Both are powerful. Choose the one that best fits your workflow. Experiment with both. Scale what works.

## 💼 Benefits of a Multi-Step Approach

The multi-step approach that Twain uses offers several key advantages:

1. **Better structure**: By breaking the generation process into discrete steps, the AI focuses on one task at a time, producing more coherent results
2. **Improved personalization**: Each step can incorporate different aspects of your prospect data
3. **Easier editing**: If you don't like a particular part of the message, you can regenerate just that section
4. **Sequence consistency**: Ensures your follow-up messages maintain a consistent voice and approach

## 🌟 Real-World Applications

Beyond basic outbound sequences, you can use Twain for:

- Multi-touch campaigns that gradually build on previous messages
- Post-event follow-up sequences
- Re-engagement campaigns for dormant prospects
- Content distribution sequences that share different resources over time
