## Clay Inbound Automation: End-to-End Workflow

## ⚒️ Building End-to-End Inbound Workflows: The Complete Revenue Engine

We're diving into a real-world example of how Clay allows you to build a powerful inbound engine that transforms form submissions into qualified, enriched leads ready for your sales team.

We'll walk through a high-level overview of an end-to-end inbound workflow and then discuss how to reverse-engineer it so you can see the big picture.

Don't worry if this seems overwhelming right now. In each lesson of this course, we'll go over every step of this workflow in detail.

## ✍🏻 Example Workflow:

1. First, an inbound lead comes in via Typeform - in this form, the lead just has to fill in their name and email address
2. These leads are then sent to a table via Webhook in Clay - we’ll cover over how to set this up in detail in our next lesson
3. Next, we begin filling in the missing data. The first enrichment fills in those corner pieces, scraping using those identifiers we collected (lead name and email) to find information like company domain that we can use to unlock more enrichments
4. Then, we go deep, finding relevant firmographic data that might help us determine whether a lead is a good fit - data points like employee headcount across departments, tech stack, company description, funding stage — all the stuff you’d usually have to Google
5. As another key part of determining fit and lead scoring, we then enrich the contacts with data (title, past experiences, time at the company)
6. All that enriched data is cleaned and normalized for use
7. Based on all this helpful data, the leads are scored based on custom criteria
8. If they pass the bar, personalized AI copy is generated based on data we’ve already collected
9. Using our native lead routing capability, leads are routed to the correct AEs with custom weightings
10. Lastly, data is exported out to the CRM or directly to your sequencing tools. fully enriched and ready to go

## 🧠 Computational Thinking with an Inbound Lens

To build more intelligent inbound workflows, apply a computational thinking approach to systematize your logic.

- First, decompose the problem. What qualifies whether this inbound lead is a good prospect for your software? Is it the size of the company, its growth rate, the industry it’s in, technology adoption patterns?
- Then, you look for patterns. What trends have you seen in your customer and conversion data? Maybe marketing leaders at enterprise-size companies operating across multiple regions are your ideal buyer personas. Perhaps fast-growing SaaS startups with remote teams are three times more likely to choose your platform.
- Next, you synthesize these insights into reusable logic. Your goal is to create a dynamic process that can run automatically and improve over time. Your inbound workflow becomes a systematic approach to identifying and qualifying inbound prospects flowing in from any channel at scale.

This is computational thinking applied to inbound. You're not just collecting data on inbound leads but building an intelligent system that allows you to get to every lead faster with the most relevant, timely context.
