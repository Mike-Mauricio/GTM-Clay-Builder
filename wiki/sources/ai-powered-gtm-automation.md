## Overview

Clay's advanced course on leveraging AI across the full GTM workflow. Introduces the FETC framework (Find, Enrich, Transform, Create) — evolving [[fete-framework|FETE]]'s "Export" into "Create" to reflect AI's ability to generate deliverables, not just move data. Covers new enrichment integrations (Perplexity, Oakie AI, image analysis), advanced personalization (AI Snippets, Twain sequences), and Claygent Builder for centralized agent management.

Builds on [[clay-101-gtm-automation|Clay 101]] and [[limitless-research|Limitless Research]]. Some lessons recap prior content — net-new content is flagged below.

## Key Takeaways

1. **FETC replaces FETE for AI-powered workflows.** The "Create" step reflects that AI doesn't just move data to other systems — it generates deliverables (sequences, battle cards, personalized messages).
2. **Claygent Builder centralizes agent management.** Build once with Sculptor, test free, deploy across tables. One change propagates everywhere.
3. **AI Snippets enable modular personalization.** Generate sentence-level content pieces (subject lines, intros, PS lines), then assemble them into complete messages — more control than full-message generation.
4. **New enrichment integrations expand the toolkit.** Perplexity (web research with citations), Oakie AI (SEC filings), Analyze Image (visual data), Twain (multi-step sequences).
5. **Company Lookalikes automate expansion.** Every closed-won deal triggers 10 lookalike companies — a self-expanding pipeline.
6. **AI ICP Search inverts list building.** Instead of manually defining filters, input your domain and let AI determine your ICP.
7. **Metaprompter + BYOK keys dramatically reduce costs.** 90% of Clay users bring their own API keys. Metaprompter writes better prompts before you spend credits.
