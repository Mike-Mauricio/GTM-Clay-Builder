# Signals & ABM — Source Summary

**Source:** Clay University course (7 lessons)

**Topic:** Signal orchestration, account-based marketing, and intelligence-driven advertising in Clay

## Course Overview

Covers real-time signal detection, custom signal building, multi-signal orchestration, and account-based marketing. Moves GTM from reactive prospecting to proactive opportunity capture. Introduces the three-table ABM architecture used internally at Clay.

## Key Concepts Introduced

- **Signal categories** — intent, growth, change, distress (4 types with different timing characteristics)
- **Default vs custom signals** — pre-built monitoring vs user-defined proprietary intelligence
- **Signal orchestration** — combining multiple signals for holistic intent view (aka "signal stacking")
- **Signal velocity** — temporal relationship between signals as an intent indicator
- **Sequential vs parallel processing** — journey-based vs threshold-based signal handling
- **Signal weight assignment** — not all signals carry equal predictive power
- **ABM stages** — aware → interested → evaluating with distinct tactics per stage
- **Three-table ABM architecture** — named accounts, brand mentions, lookup & stage updates
- **Signal prioritization matrix** — frequency × conversion potential for signal selection
- **Signal validation checklist** — 5 questions to validate custom signal candidates
